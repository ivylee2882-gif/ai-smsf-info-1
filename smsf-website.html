Why This Website Exists

This website was created for people with zero knowledge about Self-Managed Super Funds (SMSFs) to quickly understand the fundamentals. We provide clear, concise information to help you make informed decisions about your superannuation future.

Our mission is to demystify SMSFs and provide accessible information for everyday Australians considering taking control of their super.

History of SMSFs in Australia

Self-Managed Super Funds were introduced in Australia in the 1990s as part of superannuation reforms. They've grown from a niche option to managing over $800 billion in assets today.

SMSFs allow individuals to take control of their retirement savings, making investment decisions while complying with superannuation laws.

Who Can Set Up an SMSF?

Understanding eligibility and requirements for SMSF establishment

Can anyone set up an SMSF?

Generally yes, but you must be eligible to be a trustee, not be disqualified, and comply with member limits.

ATO Eligibility Guide

How many members can an SMSF have?

Maximum of 6 members, increased from 4 in 2021. All members must be trustees or directors.

ATO Member Requirements

Can children be SMSF members?

Yes, but children under 18 need a parent/guardian as their legal personal representative as trustee.

SMSF Association Guide

What disqualifies someone from being a trustee?

Bankruptcy, criminal convictions, civil penalties under superannuation law, and being disqualified by ATO or court.

ATO Disqualification Rules

Can overseas residents be SMSF members?

Complex rules apply. Generally possible but with restrictions on central management, control, and residency tests.

ATO Residency Rules

Why Set Up an SMSF?

Benefits and motivations for choosing self-managed superannuation

What are the main benefits of SMSFs?

Control over investments, potential cost savings for larger balances, estate planning flexibility, and direct property investment.

SMSF Benefits Overview

When does an SMSF become cost-effective?

Generally recommended for balances over $200,000-$500,000, depending on investment strategy and administrative costs.

ATO Cost Considerations

Can SMSFs invest in property?

Yes, including residential and commercial property, but strict rules apply regarding related party transactions.

ATO Property Investment Rules

What investment flexibility do SMSFs offer?

Wide range including shares, property, term deposits, but must comply with investment restrictions.

ATO Investment Options

How do SMSFs help with estate planning?

Greater control over death benefits, binding nominations, and succession planning for family wealth.

Estate Planning Guide

How to Set Up an SMSF?

Step-by-step process for establishing your SMSF

What are the first steps to set up an SMSF?

Establish corporate or individual trustees, create trust deed, register with ATO, obtain ABN and TFN.

ATO Setup Guide

Do I need professional help to set up an SMSF?

While not mandatory, professional advice is highly recommended for legal compliance and optimal structure setup.

Find SMSF Specialists

What documents are required for SMSF setup?

Trust deed, trustee resolutions, investment strategy, and various ATO registration forms and applications.

ATO Document Checklist

How long does SMSF setup take?

Typically 2-6 weeks for registration, longer if transferring existing super balances from other funds.

Setup Timeframes

What are the setup costs for an SMSF?

Initial costs range from $1,000-$5,000 including legal, accounting, and registration fees depending on complexity.

SMSF Cost Calculator

How to Manage an SMSF?

Ongoing responsibilities and management requirements

What are trustees' ongoing responsibilities?

Investment decisions, record keeping, annual returns, audits, compliance with superannuation and tax laws.

Trustee Responsibilities

How often must SMSFs be audited?

Annually by an approved SMSF auditor. Audit must be completed before lodging annual return.

SMSF Audit Requirements

What records must SMSFs maintain?

Financial records, investment decisions, member records, minutes of trustee meetings for minimum 10 years.

Record Keeping Requirements

When are SMSF annual returns due?

By October 31 following the end of financial year, or extended deadline if using registered agent.

Annual Return Guide

What happens if SMSF becomes non-compliant?

Potential penalties, loss of tax concessions, additional tax on income and capital gains at highest rates.

Non-Compliance Consequences

Complete control over investment decisions, asset allocation, and timing of transactions within regulatory limits.

Direct property investment including residential and commercial real estate, not available in retail funds.

Top 10 Hot SMSF Court Cases

Important legal cases that have shaped SMSF law and compliance requirements.

1. Clark v. FCT (2011) - In-house Asset Rules

This landmark case established strict interpretation of in-house asset rules where Clark's SMSF acquired units in a unit trust that invested in property leased to Clark's business. The Federal Court ruled this was an in-house asset breach, establishing that indirect investments can still violate the 5% in-house asset limit. The case highlighted the need for careful structuring when SMSFs invest in entities related to members.

The decision reinforced that substance over form applies in determining in-house assets, and trustees must consider the ultimate beneficial ownership and control of investments. This case significantly impacted how SMSFs structure property investments and related party transactions.
